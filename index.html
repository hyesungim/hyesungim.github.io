About Me

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the Maxwell Institute for Mathematical Sciences, Heriot-Watt University, supervised by Prof. Lorenzo Pareschi. My research focuses on particle-based optimisation methods, their mean-field limits, and applications to reinforcement learning.

Before starting my PhD, I completed a Master’s degree in Mathematics at Queen Mary University of London, where I worked with Dr. Linus Wunderlich on applying Deep Galerkin methods to Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman (HJB) equations. I received Distinction marks in all courses, including my dissertation.

My academic path began at Seoul National University, where I developed broad interests in mathematics. I conducted an undergraduate research internship with Prof. Insuk Seo on universality in random matrix theory, and wrote my Bachelor’s thesis on Hölder regularity for functions in the De Giorgi class, supervised by Prof. Sunsig Byun, involving analysis of elliptic PDEs.

Projects

  • Stochastic Particle Optimisation

    Particle-based optimisation methods, or metaheuristics, use interacting agents to balance exploration and exploitation when searching complex, non-convex landscapes. Examples include Genetic Algorithms, Particle Swarm Optimisation, Simulated Annealing, and Consensus-Based Optimisation. Their study often relies on mean-field limits and propagation of chaos, which provide insight into stability, convergence, and algorithmic behaviour. I am interested in applying these stochastic particle models to machine learning, where evolutionary and interaction-based dynamics offer robust alternatives, and developing their theoretical foundations in this context.

    Currently working on a mean-field framework for neural network population-based training.

    Swarm-based optimization with jumps: a kinetic BGK framework and convergence analysis , G. Borghi, H. Im and L. Pareschi [arXiv:2507.00871]

    Status: Published in Communications on Pure and Applied Analysis, 2025

  • Stochastic Control Problem and Deep Learning Method

    Solving high-dimensional Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman (HJB) equation with deep Galerkin method.

    The method uses neural network function approximators within a weak formulation to overcome the curse of dimensionality and enables efficient numerical approach to solving fully nonlinear HJB PDEs arising in optimal control.
