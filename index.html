About Me

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the Maxwell Institute for Mathematical Sciences, Heriot-Watt University, supervised by Prof. Lorenzo Pareschi. My research focuses on particle-based optimisation methods, their mean-field limits, and applications to reinforcement learning.

Before starting my PhD, I completed a Master’s degree in Mathematics at Queen Mary University of London, where I worked with Dr. Linus Wunderlich on applying Deep Galerkin methods to Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman (HJB) equations. I received Distinction marks in all courses, including my dissertation.

My academic path began at Seoul National University, where I developed broad interests in mathematics. I conducted an undergraduate research internship with Prof. Insuk Seo on universality in random matrix theory, and wrote my Bachelor’s thesis on Hölder regularity for functions in the De Giorgi class, supervised by Prof. Sunsig Byun, involving analysis of elliptic PDEs.
